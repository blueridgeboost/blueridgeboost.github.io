CyberSec Explorers - Unleash Your Digital Superpowers!

Are you a middle school student with a passion for technology and a desire to protect your digital world? Look no further! Our cutting-edge labs are specifically designed to introduce you to the exciting and ever-growing field of cybersecurity.

At CyberSec Explorers, we have two primary goals in mind. First and foremost, we want to equip you with the knowledge and skills to navigate the digital landscape safely and ethically. Through immersive hands-on activities, we'll teach you the essential principles of digital hygiene and ethical computing use. You'll learn how to safeguard your personal information, protect your devices from malicious threats, and become a responsible digital citizen.
But it doesn't stop there! Our labs go beyond basic cybersecurity practices. We take you on an exhilarating journey into the mind of an illegal hacker. You'll explore the tactics and techniques they commonly employ to breach networks and compromise devices. Don't worry, though – we'll also reveal the secrets of cybersecurity professionals and the ingenious steps they take to stop these attacks in their tracks.

Unit 1: Ethics and Networks

Lab 1: The Ethics of Computing: Right vs. Wrong

We all have a sense of right versus wrong, but decisions can become more complex when computers and technology are involved. Unfortunately, the unethical use of computers can harm ourselves and others in our community and society. A strong code of ethics forms the heart of cybersecurity, influencing the choices we make when using our computing devices as well as how we protect ourselves and others from cyber threats. In this lab, get started on your cybersecurity journey by exploring the ethical and unethical uses of computers and making them come to life with BOLT programs.

Lab 2: How the Internet Works: The Big Picture

Most of us use the internet daily for work, school, and life. But what exactly is the internet and how does it work? In this lab, you’ll set up a model for the internet and explore how TCP/IP protocols control how computers send and receive information around the world in a matter of seconds.

Lab 3: Private Networks: Keeping it Private

Discover how a modem and router work together to establish a private local area network (LAN) while exploring the significance of firewalls in safeguarding your network in this lab. Although the internet is an expansive public space, chances are you primarily access it through a private LAN, whether you're at home, the library, or school.

Lab 4: Our Internet Tracks: Digital Footprints

Digital footprints, unlike footprints on the ground, can have lasting implications as they can be used to track your online activities and choices. It's crucial to be mindful of this from the moment you begin using the internet. In this lab, you will delve into the concept of digital footprints and understand their impact on various aspects of your life.

Lab 5: Our Internet Tracks: Digital Footprints

To safeguard your personal information, it is vital to comprehend the creation and utilization of your digital footprint. Although some aspects of your digital footprint can be innocuous or even beneficial, without proper attention, it can rapidly accumulate a wealth of information that you may not wish to be accessible to others. In this lab, you will investigate the nature of cookies, their purpose on the internet, and methods to manage and control your digital footprint.

Unit 2: Cyberbullies and hackers

Lab 1: Don't Be a Bully: Stopping Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying, a term that you are likely familiar with and may have personally encountered, unfortunately, pervades many aspects of our daily technology use, such as social media, texting, and gaming. The impact of cyberbullying is profoundly detrimental, affecting not only the victim but also the individual engaging in such behavior. In this lab, we will delve into the definition of cyberbullying, the repercussions it can have, and strategies to prevent its occurrence.

Lab 2: Types of Hackers: To Hack or Not to Hack

The cybersecurity realm encompasses a diverse array of hackers, ranging from the benevolent to the malicious. In this lab, you will gain insight into the various categories of hackers and explore their motivations. Additionally, you will engage in a game that simulates the interaction between different cybersecurity actors, with some attempting to breach a school's data system while others endeavor to defend it.

Lab 3: Phishing: How Illegal Hackers Find You

In today's age, our daily routines involve extensive interactions with apps, websites, and other digital platforms on the internet. However, as our lives and valuable information are increasingly present online, some individuals seek to exploit this data to their advantage. Phishing and social engineering are common techniques employed to deceive unsuspecting individuals into willingly divulging their personal information, which can then be used to gain unauthorized access to passwords, bank accounts, credit card details, and more. In this lab, you will explore various methods employed by individuals attempting to acquire personally identifiable information (PII) and learn techniques to detect and prevent falling victim to such attacks. By developing these skills, you can safeguard yourself against potential threats.

Lab 4: Virus: Modeling the Spread of a Computer Infection

Malware, which refers to malicious software created to harm and disrupt our technology, has become an unfortunate reality in our modern lives. Nevertheless, understanding its nature and functioning can empower us to better protect our computers and ourselves. In this lab, you will experiment to comprehend how a specific type of malware, namely a virus, can propagate from one BOLT to another. Subsequently, you will devise a patch, or a fix, to ensure the security of your BOLT device, thereby enhancing its safety.

Lab 5: The Morris Worm: Historical Malware

Similar to a physical worm, computer worms possess the ability to propagate, spread, and proliferate throughout a network, infecting computers in their path. While some worms may be harmless, many are designed as malicious malware that can inflict damage on computers and create vulnerabilities for potential attacks. In this lab, you will embark on a comprehensive exploration of the history of the very first computer worm. Additionally, you will have the opportunity to experience the effects of this worm through a BOLT program, providing you with a practical understanding of its impact.

Unit 3: CIA triad and attacks

Lab 1: CIA Triad: Planning for Security

When it comes to information and network security planning, cybersecurity professionals prioritize the CIA Triad, which consists of three fundamental principles: Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability. In this lab, you will delve into the meaning and significance of each principle within the context of cybersecurity. By exploring these concepts, you will gain a comprehensive understanding of how they shape decision-making processes in ensuring the security of information and networks.

Lab 2: Hacker Snooping: Sniffing and Scanning

Before hackers can launch an attack, they typically gather information about your vulnerabilities. Cybercriminals employ two reconnaissance techniques, namely sniffing and scanning, to identify potential weaknesses. In this lab, you will explore the concepts of sniffing and scanning, understanding how they are used by cybercriminals to gather information. Additionally, you will engage in a game where you compete against another team to take over and control their BOLT device. This lab builds upon the concepts of Confidentiality and Integrity introduced in the CIA Triad lab, allowing you to further develop your understanding of these principles in a practical setting.

Lab 3: Denied: Denial of Service Attacks

Denial of Service (DoS) and Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks are prevalent and highly disruptive forms of cyberattacks. Even relatively inexperienced cybercriminals can effectively target and bring down large websites, rendering information and services inaccessible to businesses and computer users. In this lab, you will simulate a DoS attack using two BOLTS, gaining insights into the mechanics of such an attack. Furthermore, you will explore strategies and measures that organizations can employ to safeguard themselves against these types of attacks, ensuring the availability and functionality of their systems and services.

Lab 4: Person-in-the-Middle Attacks: Who's There?

A person-in-the-middle attack refers to an illegal hacking technique where an unauthorized individual intercepts communications between you and the internet services you use while on a computer, enabling them to eavesdrop or manipulate the data transmitted. In this lab, you will simulate and investigate a person-in-the-middle attack using BOLT robots. By doing so, you will gain a practical understanding of how such attacks can occur. Additionally, you will learn effective strategies to protect yourself and mitigate the risks associated with person-in-the-middle attacks, ensuring your online safety and privacy.

Lab 5: Circles of Influence - Authenticate & Authorize

In the realm of user management, different types of users require distinct levels of authorization to access apps, websites, and networks. When this authorization is compromised, it can lead to chaotic consequences. In this lab, students will explore the reasons why varying levels of access are necessary for users, administrators, and developers in the context of cybersecurity. Through this lab, students will gain a deeper understanding of user authentication and its vital role in ensuring secure access to digital resources.

Unit 4: Cryptography and your future

Lab 1: p@$$W0RD$!: What’s Behind a Strong Password

When it comes to cracking passwords, computers are significantly more powerful than humans. Their computational speed and efficiency enable them to perform password-cracking attempts at a much faster rate than humans can. In this lab, you will program BOLT to simulate the process of cracking a combination lock, providing you with a practical understanding of the techniques employed by malicious actors to compromise accounts when passwords are not sufficiently secure. By engaging in this lab, you will gain insights into the importance of strong passwords and the potential risks associated with weak or easily guessable passwords.

Lab 2: Intro to Cryptography: Pigpen Cipher

Yes, just like humans passing notes in a secret code, computers also communicate through encrypted messages. In this lab, you will delve into the fascinating world of cryptography, the practice of writing and decoding codes. You will learn about different cryptographic techniques and their significance in securing information. Furthermore, you will use BOLT and the pigpen cipher, a simple substitution cipher, to send and receive secret messages with your classmates. This lab will provide you with hands-on experience in cryptography and an opportunity to explore the art of secure communication.

Lab 3: The Caesar Shift: Caesar Says What?

Indeed, Julius Caesar was known to encrypt his communications, and one of the most famous ciphers, the Caesar Shift, is named after him. This cipher involves shifting the letters of the alphabet by a certain number of positions to create an encrypted message. In this lab, you will further your understanding of cryptography by utilizing BOLT to explore the Caesar Cipher. By experimenting with different shift values, you will encrypt and decrypt messages using this classic encryption technique. This lab will provide you with hands-on experience in working with the Caesar Cipher and deepen your knowledge of cryptographic methods.

Lab 4: Multiply It! Modulo Arithmetic & Ciphers

In this lab, you’ll learn about the multiplication cipher, a monoalphabetic cipher. In the process you’ll become comfortable with modular arithmetic and begin to understand its importance to modern cryptography.

Lab 5: Cybersecurity Career Parade

Incident responders, cryptographers, penetration testers...One of these could be your job title in the future. In this lab, you’ll learn about a cybersecurity career and then design an avatar to share what you’ve learned with classmates in a cybersecurity career parade!
